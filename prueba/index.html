Biography


Sergio Maestre was born on July 18th of 1994, Caracas, Venezuela. At the age of 5 years old he started studying in the one of the best schools in Venezuela, called, "Colegio Emil Friedman". Two months after, he felt really attracted to music so he decided to try some instruments. After the Trumpet, Trombone, Clarinet, Bassoon, etc; he was really into Percussion. The feeling was so strong that he stayed with that instrument.

Sergio started playing percussion in this first orchestra called “Ensamble Lido Guarnieri” with his Master and first teacher, Rosanna Rodriguez. After a couple of years he was already playing in more than three orchestras in the School and more than two out of school, “Orquestra Simon Bolivar” and “Orquesta Juvenil de Caracas”. He has taken classes with amazing percussionists Ricardo Alvarado, Dennis Fallas, Miguel Marquez, Ulises Ascanio, Gustavo Dudamel and many others. He has played with many bands and musicians, for example; Ensamble Gurrufio, Ernesto Laya, Cheo Hurtado, El Pollo Brito, C4 Trio, Andres Cepeda, Sir Simon Rattle, The Berlin Orchestra, 15 of the Best Percussionist in Venezuela, Percussion Ensamble, Venezuelan Percussion Ensemble, Chino y Nacho, and many others.

In 2012 he graduated from high school and decided to go to Berklee College of Music, but it wasn’t until 2013 when he officially started it.

Although hand percussion is his first instrument, he also brought his traditional instrument and cultural music to this College (Maracas and Joropo).

He has taken classes with Ernesto Diaz, Ricardo Monzon, Mark Walker and Eguie Castrillo. He has played at the Berklee Performance Center (BPC), Regatta Bar in Cambridge, Providence, Rhode Island, Elizabeth Garner Museum, Symphony Hall and many other places since being at Berklee, and has shared the stage with notorious musicians such as Paquito D’ Rivera, Oscar Stagnaro, Mark Walker, Diego Urcola, Alex Brown, Eguie Castrillo, Paulo Stagnaro, Tia Fuller, Geri Allen, Shamie Royston, Rebecca Cline, and Brian Mckinght.

Presently, he is in charge with Stefano Melillo in Projects in Venezuela trying to create a relation between Musician in Venezuela and Berklee College of Music. Projects as “Berklee en el Friedman” and “Puentes” have been a success in Venezuela leaving the name of Berklee College of Music in a big level. He is working with Emilio Mendez in their project called Berklee Latin All Stars after they started it with Rebecca Cline. They are taking the project out to Puerto Rico and Dominican Republic. Sergio is in his 4th semester at Berklee College of Music, dual majoring in Performance and Music Business and minoring in Psychology.
